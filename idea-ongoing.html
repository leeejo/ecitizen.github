Good Neighbours Video

Posted on 5 May 2015

  • Overview
  • Guidelines
  • Terms and Conditions

Overview

What does a good neighbour mean to you?

The Good Neighbours Video Competition was launched during HDB Community Week 2014 to encourage participants to express their thoughts creatively through a video recording on the theme “what does a good neighbour mean to you?”

The competition was held from 24 May to 30 Nov 2014, with close to 4,000 video entries submitted. These videos expressed neighbourliness through various ways such as story-telling, animation and acting. 13 entries were selected for the top 3 and 10 merit prizes. The public could also vote for their favourite video among all the shortlisted entries from 6 to 27 Mar 2015. The video with the highest number of votes will receive the Most Popular Video Award.

Guidelines

  1. To enter the competition, participants shall submit their video entries between 24 May 2014 (12pm) and 30 Nov 2014 (11.59pm) by either sending the entry via a cloud storage service (e.g. WeTransfer, Dropbox) to good_neighbour@mailbox.hdb.gov.sg or delivering a copy to the Roving Exhibition: Stories on Wheels.
  2. Participants can join as an individual or in a group.
  3. Entries should meet the following requirements:
    • Recording in a video format (e.g. MPEG4 in H.264, DivX or Xvid)
    • a video format (e.g. MPEG4 in H.264, DivX or Xvid)
    • Recorded video must be less than or up to one minute in length
    • The entry must be an original composition which is unpublished and has not been performed before. Sound recordings and images incorporated into your video should be your original works. Remember copyrighted materials like sound recordings, music, graphics, and still or video images cannot be used without permissions or through licensing agreements
    • All participants are required to include their full name (as in NRIC/ Passport) and video title upon submission
    • Shortlisted participants must complete a declaration form; filling in their personal particulars in the compulsory fields (i.e. marked with asterisks) and declaring that they have written consent from people featured or identifiable in their video(s)
  4. By submitting entries, participants have acknowledged that they have read, understood and agreed to Terms and Conditions
