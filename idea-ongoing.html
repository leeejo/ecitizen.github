Overview

What does a Good Neighbour mean to you?

The Good Neighbours Video Competition allows participants to share endearing stories of their neighbourly experiences by expressing themselves creatively through a short video clip. The message captured in the video clip also serves to inspire and motivate the community to get involved in making their neighbourhoods a better place to live in.

Have a story to share about neighbourliness? Express yourself creatively on video and submit your entries today!

Create a video in any form (Maximum time of 1 minute), for example:

  • Tell us a compelling STORY about a good neighbour you know
  • Perform an original SONG/DANCE about what being a good neighbour means to you
  • Shoot a SHORT FILM about good neighbours
  • Present yourself in any other expressions such as STOP-MOTION VIDEO or SHADOW PUPPETRY to demonstrate neighbourliness

What’s more, stand a chance to win attractive prizes with your submission!

Rules and Regulations

  1. Submission of entries begins on 24 May 2015 at 12pm and ends on 30 Nov 2015 at 11.59pm. Entries received after the abovementioned period shall not be considered.
  2. Participants can send the entry via the “Submit Your Idea” button on the right.
  3. Each participant can submit more than one video entry.
  4. Participants may take part as an individual or as a group.

Eligibility and Entry Procedures

  1. Any participant who has not attained 18 years of age and above (based on his/her date-of-birth at the time of the submission of his/her entries to the competition) must obtain his/her parental/guardian’s consent to participate in the competition. By submitting an entry, any participant under the age of 18 represents and warrants that he/she has obtained his/her parental/guardian’s consent. HDB reserves the right to disqualify any participant who does not comply with this term from the competition.
  2. All participants are required to include a video title and short description of their video during submission. Any entry with incomplete or invalid details will be disqualified.

Video Requirements

  1. Entries should meet the following requirements:
    • Recorded in video format.
    • Recorded in English, Mandarin, Malay, Tamil or any of Singapore’s dialects.
    • Recorded video must be less than or up to 1 minute in length
    • The entry must be an original composition which is unpublished and has not been performed before. Sound recordings and images incorporated into your video should be your original works. Remember copyrighted materials like sound recordings, music, graphics, and still or video images cannot be used without permissions or through licensing agreements.
    • All participants are required to include a video title and short description of their video upon submission.
    • Shortlisted participants must complete a declaration form; filling in their personal particulars in the compulsory fields (i.e. marked with asterisks) and declaring that they have written consent from people featured or identifiable in their video(s).

  2. We recommend that you compress your video before submission to reduce its file size, allowing a faster upload and smooth playback online. You can follow the guidelines below. However, it is important to ensure that the video is not compressed too much, to avoid losing its detail, resolution and clarity.

    File Format and Codec

    We recommend an mp4 format and the following whenever possible:

    • H.264
    • Progressive scan (no interlacing)
    • Closed GOP. GOP of half the frame rate.
    • CABAC

    Frame rate: 24, 25, or 30 FPS

    Video clips should be submitted with frame rates of 24 (or 23.976), 25, and 30 (or 29.97). It is recommended to encode your final video at the same frame rate of your footage. For example, content recorded in 720p60, should be uploaded at 720p60. In the case of content at 1080i60, they should be de-interlaced to 30 progressive frames per second. Always choose “constant” instead of “variable” frame rate.

    Resolution: 640 (SD) or 720 pixels wide (HD)

    Standard Definition (SD) videos include resolutions of 640 x 360 px (16:9 aspect ratio) and 1280 x 720 px (16:9 aspect ratio) for HD videos. We would advise you to encode at these specifications to ensure that your video can be played properly on screen.

    Bit rate: 1,000 – 15,000 kbit/s (SD) / 5,000 – 30,000 kbit/s (HD)

    The data rate, otherwise known as bit rate determines the visual quality and file size of your submission. It is best if you set the target as detailed below.

    Standard Quality Uploads
    Type Video Bitrate Mono Audio Bitrate Stereo Audio Bitrate 5.1 Audio Bitrate
    720p 5,000 kbps 128 kbps 384 kbps 512 kbps
    480p 2,500 kbps 64 kbps 128 kbps 196 kbps
    360p 1,000 kbps 64 kbps 128 kbps 196 kbps
    High Quality Uploads
    Type Video Bitrate Mono Audio Bitrate Stereo Audio Bitrate 5.1 Audio Bitrate
    720p 30,000 kbps 128 kbps 384 kbps 512 kbps
    480p 15,000 kbps 128 kbps 384 kbps 512 kbps
    360p 5,000 kbps 128 kbps 384 kbps 512 kbps

    Audio

    For optimum results, choose an appropriate sample rate (48 or 96 khz) and channel (Stereo or Stereo +5.1)

Judging Criteria

  1. The shortlisting and judging criteria will be based on the following:
    • Relevance to the theme of neighbourliness (40%)
    • Degree of motivation and inspiration to the community (30%)
    • Creativity and content (Interactivity, delivery) (30%)
  2. Shortlisted entries will be put up for public voting in Dec 2015.
  3. The decision of the judges is final and no appeals will be accepted or entertained.
  4. All winners will be issued certificates of participation in addition to the cash prizes.
  5. HDB reserves the right to substitute or replace the prize with any other prizes of similar value without prior notice.
  6. Prizes are non-transferable, non-negotiable and non-exchangeable.
  7. Submitted entries with content deemed as irrelevant or offensive by HDB will be removed at HDB’s discretion.

FAQ

Video Competition

  1. What is the Good Neighbours Video Competition about?

    The Good Neighbours Video Competition allows participants to share endearing stories of their neighbourly experiences by expressing themselves creatively through a short video clip. The message captured in the video clip also serves to inspire and motivate the community to get involved in making their neighbourhoods a better place to live in.

  2. Who is eligible to take part?

    Singapore citizens and permanent residents of all ages are eligible. For participants below the age of 18, you are considered to have obtained parental consent upon submission of your video entries.

  3. When is the submission period?

    Submission of entries begins on 24 May 2015 at 12pm and ends on 30 Nov 2015 at 11.59pm. Entries received after 30 Nov 2015 will not be considered.

  4. Can I record a song in languages other than English?

    Yes, entries in Mandarin, Malay, Tamil or any of Singapore’s dialects are accepted.

  5. Can the video submission be longer than 1 minute?

    Video submissions must be less than or up to 1 minute in length. Any video submissions which are more than 1 minute will not be eligible for the competition.

  6. Are group entries allowed?

    Yes, you may take part as an individual or as a group.

  7. How do I submit my entry?

    You may refer to Rules and Regulations for details on how to submit an entry.

  8. Can I submit more than 1 video?

    Yes, there are no restrictions to the number of video entries you can upload.

  9. How are the entries shortlisted and evaluated?

    The shortlisting and evaluation of qualifying entries will be based on the following criteria:

    • 40% for Relevance to the theme of neighbourliness
    • 30% for Degree of motivation and inspiration to the community
    • 30% for Creativity and content (interactivity, delivery)

  10. How else can I participate in the contest if I don’t have a video camera?

    You can come onboard our mobile recording studio “Roving Exhibition: Stories on Wheels” at the following locations to shoot and submit the video.

    You can also participate in the website voting, which will start in Dec 2015.

Uploading

  1. Can I upload a video in High Definition (HD)?

    Yes.

  2. How can I ensure that my videos will be uploaded successfully?

    Upon successfully receiving your video file, video title and short description of your video, an acknowledgement receipt with a unique serial number will be sent to your registered email address used during submission. Alternatively, the serial number will be enclosed in your souvenir when you come onboard the Roving Exhibition: Stories on Wheels.

    Do allow up to 10 working days thereafter to view your submissions online.
